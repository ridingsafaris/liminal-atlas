Biography

Milton William “Bill” Cooper (May 6, 1943 – November 5, 2001) was a Navy veteran, radio broadcaster, and author whose 1991 book Behold a Pale Horse became one of the most widely circulated underground texts in American history — one of the most shoplifted books in the country, mandatory reading in prison cellblocks, and a foundational text for hip-hop culture, the militia movement, and what would eventually become the QAnon framework.

He served in the US Navy and claims he was attached to the CINCPACFLT (Commander in Chief Pacific Fleet) Intelligence Briefing Team, where he read Top Secret documents about alien treaties, MJ-12, and a secret government. His documented rank was E-5 (petty officer second class) — incompatible with the clearances he described. He acknowledged mixing true and fabricated information in his public output and could not always distinguish which was which.

From 1992 to 2001 he broadcast The Hour of the Time via shortwave radio from Eagar, Arizona, covering every major conspiracy theme of the era. On June 28, 2001 he warned listeners that a major attack would be blamed on Osama bin Laden — three months before 9/11. He was shot and killed on November 5, 2001 when Apache County sheriff’s deputies attempted to arrest him on outstanding charges. He had publicly vowed he would not be taken alive.

His influence on the figures and ideas covered on Liminal Atlas is indirect but pervasive: the MJ-12 framework, the alien treaty, the secret government with unaccountable black budgets, the breakaway civilisation, FEMA emergency powers, and the deep state — these are Cooper’s architecture. He did not originate all of these ideas, but he synthesised and circulated them more widely than any other single source in the 1990s.
