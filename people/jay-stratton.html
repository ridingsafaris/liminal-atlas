Overview

Jay Stratton is a Defense Intelligence Agency officer who, together with Jim Lacatski, co-founded and built the OSAP (Office of the Under Secretary of Defense for Intelligence) UAP programme. He was involved in OSAP from its inception, not just as its later director. He subsequently directed the UAP Task Force — the successor programme that conducted the formal investigation whose public existence was acknowledged in 2020.

Eric Davis worked directly with Stratton and Lacatski through the AATIP/OSAP framework. Grusch (NRO) was Stratton’s liaison on the task force. Davis credits Stratton with having a general awareness of the crash retrieval programme and of Lacatski’s experiences, though the full scope of what Stratton knows remains classified.

The creation of OSAP and the UAP Task Force under Stratton and Lacatski is widely credited as the institutional foundation that eventually led to the 2017 New York Times disclosure, Grusch’s 2023 congressional testimony, and the current wave of public UAP discussion.
