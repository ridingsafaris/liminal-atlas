Biography

Air conditioning magnate who funded Brown’s experimental gravity work and held the 1957 Chapel Hill Conference on gravitation (attended by Feynman, Wheeler, and the world’s top theoretical physicists) — sponsored by Wright-Patterson Air Force Base. Per the 1971 Australian JIO intelligence document, his Institute for Field Physics at UNC Chapel Hill was effectively a CIA academic satellite tasked with studying anti-gravity. Wrote in his notes: ‘There is something going on here that we are unable to account for.’ In 1959 wrote the sci-fi novel The Stars Are Too High about a fake alien invasion by rogue anti-gravity scientists using Nazi-derived flying saucer technology.
