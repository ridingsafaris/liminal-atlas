Overview

David Grusch served as a United States Air Force officer and intelligence official. He was the NRO (National Reconnaissance Office) liaison officer to Jay Stratton’s UAP Task Force, working alongside Travis Taylor and others in a small core group of around 40 people (with a broader peripheral body of around 1,000 contributors). Eric Davis was among those who knew Grusch at the Aerospace Corporation in Colorado Springs, where Grusch was working for the NRO customer occupying floors of that building.

In 2021, Grusch filed a classified complaint with the Intelligence Community Inspector General alleging the existence of a multi-decade UAP crash retrieval and reverse engineering programme concealed from Congressional oversight. The complaint was assessed as credible and urgent. In June 2023, Grusch testified before the House Oversight Committee, stating under oath that he had spoken with individuals with firsthand knowledge of non-human origin craft and biologics.

Davis notes that of Grusch’s approximately 40 firsthand witnesses, not a single one was a theoretical physicist. All were engineers, materials scientists, electrical engineers, or aerospace engineers. Davis and Weinstein use this observation as the anchor for the episode’s central thesis.

Grusch later became COO of the Sol Foundation, the UAP research organisation co-founded by Gary Nolan at Stanford.
