Biography

Biographer who spent years tracking Brown’s surviving colleagues, daughter Linda, and FOIA records. Surfaced the 1943 FBI file stating Brown knew more about radar detection than any individual in the US Navy. Interviewed Jacques Cornon at age 98, just before his death. Coined the ‘Wounded Prairie Chicken Routine’ for Brown’s self-discrediting strategy. The book is the first comprehensive documented account of Brown’s life, drawing on original interviews with Linda Brown and Brown’s other surviving colleagues.
