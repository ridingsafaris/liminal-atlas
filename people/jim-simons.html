Overview

James Harris Simons (1938–2024) was one of the most consequential mathematicians and investors of the 20th century. He received his PhD in mathematics from UC Berkeley in 1961 and became chair of the mathematics department at SUNY Stony Brook, where he played a central role in building one of the most distinguished mathematics programmes in the country and where Chen-Ning Yang (Nobel Prize, 1957) was also based.

In the early 1970s, while working with Shiing-Shen Chern at MIT, Simons co-discovered the Chern-Simons differential form — a topological invariant that appears in the transgression of the Pontryagin class and has become fundamental to condensed matter physics, quantum field theory, and string theory. It is the topological term at the heart of QCD’s theta sector.

In 1978 Simons left mathematics to trade currencies and in 1982 founded Renaissance Technologies. The Medallion Fund, available only to employees, achieved sustained gross annual returns of approximately 66% from 1988 to 2018 — an anomaly that has never been satisfactorily explained by any public account of their methods. RenTech’s campus on Long Island is adjacent to Brookhaven National Laboratory. SUNY Stony Brook, with which Simons was closely associated, is described by Weinstein as having mathematics and physics presence far above its state university ranking.

Eric Weinstein confronted Simons directly on two occasions before his death in May 2024. On the first, Weinstein presented his gauge theory of economics (with Pia Malaney); Simons engaged seriously and then asked “Do you have any idea where you get the money?” despite his estimated $20B net worth. On the second, Weinstein explained that Chern-Simons is structurally the closest action in physics to Einstein-Hilbert, and that both share the property of being zeroth order in curvature when varied. Simons invited Weinstein to spend a year at the Simons Center for Geometry and Physics at Stony Brook. When Weinstein asked for modest family relocation support, Simons again deflected: “Do you have any idea where you get the money?” When Weinstein asked directly whether RenTech was using differential geometry in trading, Simons took a long drag on his cigarette and said: “Eric, if you knew how I actually made money, you’d be so disappointed.”

Weinstein’s inference: if a Manhattan 3.0 for gravity and UAPs exists, Renaissance Technologies is its brain trust with 95% confidence.
