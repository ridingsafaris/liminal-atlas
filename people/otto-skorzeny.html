Biography

Austrian SS colonel regarded as the most dangerous man in Europe during WWII. Pulled off the Gran Sasso raid to rescue Mussolini. Master of psychological warfare and special operations (Abteilung Skorzeny). Attended a demonstration of the Bell at the Schoto Works in 1944 and became obsessed with using it as a psychological warfare operation.

After the war: never formally surrendered. Key organiser of ODESSA (Order of Former SS Officers, also known as 'the Spider'), based in Argentina alongside Martin Borman. Spanish newspaper reports cited by Joseph Farrell place Skorzeny with Franco on the Mediterranean coast of Spain in 1946 launching saucer-shaped rockets toward America — one year before Roswell.
