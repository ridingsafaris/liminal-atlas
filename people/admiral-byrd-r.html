Biography

US Navy rear admiral and pioneering polar explorer. Led Operation High Jump in 1946 — one of the largest naval expeditions ever sent to Antarctica, immediately after Nazi submarine traffic was intercepted heading toward Patagonia. The expedition was planned to last months but returned early, badly damaged, with ships and personnel lost.

At a port in Chile on the way back, Byrd gave a statement to the Chilean press warning that America needed to prepare for a Third World War in which aircraft could cross from pole to pole at tremendous speeds. He was then debriefed in Washington and never spoke publicly about Antarctica again.
