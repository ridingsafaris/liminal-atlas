Glenn Gaffney
CIA · Deputy Director for Science & Technology · UAP Portfolio Owner
UAPCIAIntelligence CommunityDS&T
Overview

Glenn Gaffney served as Deputy Director for Science and Technology (DS&T) at the Central Intelligence Agency. Eric Davis identifies him as the CIA officer who held the portfolio ownership of the crash retrieval programme — functioning as the central intelligence figure overseeing the stovepiped programme segments distributed across Lockheed Martin, TRW, and other aerospace contractors.

In the UAP programme architecture, Gaffney’s role was analogous to Leslie Groves in the Manhattan Project: the central military-intelligence authority with awareness of the whole, while the individual contractors operated in compartmentalised isolation from each other. The budget for the programme ran through DIA director-level accounts under innocuous codes, which is why even senior officials like Admiral Wilson were unaware it existed despite their budgets funding it.

Davis attempted to arrange a meeting with Gaffney through official AATIP channels. Gaffney refused to engage. Davis explicitly names him in the context of his OSAP discussion — “And that was Glenn Gaffney” — when describing who the portfolio owner was during the period he was investigating.
