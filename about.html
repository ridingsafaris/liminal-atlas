What This Is

Liminal Atlas is a knowledge-sharing blog that takes seriously the questions mainstream institutions are too cautious — or too institutional — to engage with honestly. We cover frontier physics, comparative religion, UAP research, consciousness studies, and the places where these fields intersect in ways that demand attention.

Our format is the close reading: we take a podcast episode, a paper, a book, or an encounter narrative and unpack it systematically — with physics explainers, inline diagrams, links to every person mentioned on Wikipedia, and honest notes about what is verified and what is not.

We are not a conspiracy blog. We check primary sources. We link to the DIA FOIA documents, the actual patents, the peer-reviewed papers. When something is mainstream physics, we say so. When something is contested, we say so. When something is fringe, we say that too — and then we explain why it might still be worth understanding.

Editorial Standards

  • Every factual claim links to a primary source — Wikipedia, arXiv, patent filings, or FOIA documents.
  • We distinguish between mainstream consensus, contested science, and speculation. These are always labelled.
  • We do not reproduce copyrighted material. We paraphrase dialogue and note where reconstructions are approximate.
  • We do not take money from anyone we write about. No sponsored content, no affiliate physics.
  • Amazon book links are for reader convenience. We have no affiliate relationship.
  • When we get something wrong, we correct it with a visible note, not a silent edit.
  • We are not anti-science. We are pro-rigour — including rigour about what science has and hasn't actually tested.

Contact & Contributions

If you have a correction, a source we missed, or a topic you'd like to see covered — reach out. If you're a researcher in one of the fields we cover and want to contribute a piece, we're interested.

We're also building a community for people who take these questions seriously. If that's you, stay tuned.
