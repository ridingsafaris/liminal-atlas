Documents & Primary Sources

2001 · Rumsfeld · $2.3 Trillion Missing from Pentagon (Sep 10, 2001)

Secretary of Defense Donald Rumsfeld announced on September 10, 2001 — the day before 9/11 — that $2.3 trillion in Pentagon transactions could not be accounted for.

Fitts cites this as the public surface of the broader $21 trillion missing money story. She claims DoD’s actual unaccounted figure was $4 trillion, not $2.3T. The 9/11 attacks the following day buried the story entirely. She worked with a reporter on a per-taxpayer breakdown of the missing money — a story that was never published.

2020 · Agustín Carstens · CBDC Speech at IMF Panel

59-second video from a 2020 IMF panel in which Carstens, General Manager of the BIS, explicitly states that CBDCs allow central banks to make and enforce rules on how individuals spend their money.

Fitts says this is the only time she has seen a central banker tell the truth in public. She describes nearly falling off her chair when she first saw it. Available on Solari Report under “Video Shorts — Financial Transaction Freedom.”

1947 · National Security Act & 1949 · CIA Act

The National Security Act (1947) created the NSC, the CIA, and the Joint Chiefs. The CIA Act (1949) gave the CIA unprecedented financial secrecy, allowing it to transfer funds from other agency appropriations without congressional knowledge.

Fitts describes these two acts as the legal architecture of the breakaway civilisation. The CIA Act was passed, she argues, only after the assassination of James Forrestal who was blocking it. Together they created the mechanism for unlimited covert funding via the Exchange Stabilization Fund and corporate veil.

2012 · Washington Post · “Top Secret America”

Washington Post investigative series by Dana Priest and William Arkin documenting the explosion of national security infrastructure built after the Patriot Act (2001).

Fitts cites this as the closest thing to a public map of the post-9/11 black budget buildout. She describes the waste as “beyond imagination.” The series found 1,900+ private companies working on top-secret programmes and 850,000+ people with top-secret clearances.
