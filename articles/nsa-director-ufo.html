The Intelligence Analyst’s Hypothesis Matrix Applied to UAPs

📋
Chapter Summary

Eric lays out the analytical framework he brings from the CIA: the “method of competing hypotheses.” For any phenomenon, you list every possible explanation, examine evidence that supports or contradicts each, then weight them to reach a probabilistic assessment. Applied to UAPs, the hypotheses surveyed in their book run from the mundane (drones, balloons, atmospheric effects like ball lightning and sprites) through classified human-origin craft, to non-human but earthly origin, to extraterrestrial, to time-travellers from Earth’s past or future. A crucial analytical principle is introduced: “Don’t look for your keys under the lamppost” — don’t only look where you can see. The “negative space” — where you don’t expect and don’t want the answer to be — is where it likely is. Chris introduces the exopsychology dimension: systematically imagining what an extraterrestrial intelligence would be by taking everything human and assuming the opposite.

🞜 American Alchemy · ▶ 5:12 — The method of competing hypotheses
Dr. Eric Hazelton
“We start with the observer themselves. When you look at a phenomena, you have to look at what’s reporting it and how accurate and biased is the thing that’s reporting it. So the human instrument — as a neuroscientist, as a physician, we can both tell you the human instrument is highly flawed.”
Dr. Eric Hazelton
“At CIA, we used to have a saying: ‘Do not look for your keys under the lampost.’ Don’t look only where you can see. If you’re not seeing something, it’s probably because you don’t know where else to look. We have to look in the negative space.”
🞜 American Alchemy · ▶ 10:00 — Exopsychology: the anti-motivations framework
Dr. Chris Gilbert
“We talk about exopsychology — imagining what an extraterrestrial would be. It could be made of dark matter and feeding on dark energy. They might not derive from animal forms. They might not require food or water to survive. Maybe they are immortal. Maybe they don’t sexually reproduce. Maybe they have no written or spoken language. What if they are a collection of single cells that develop and live in space and not on a planet?”
Dr. Eric Hazelton
“We did the opposite of mirroring. We said let’s take everything that humans are and assume that extraterrestrials are none of those — they’re the opposite — because we would never think to look there and we never think to understand the motivation. When you do that, it’s freeing.”

Frame dragging around a spinning black hole produces closed timelike curves — a mathematically permissible form of time travel. GLOSSARY10 key terms from this episode

Technical Glossary

Method of Competing Hypotheses
The CIA’s analytical framework for assessing unknown phenomena: list all possible explanations, examine evidence supporting or contradicting each, then weight to produce a probabilistic assessment. Hazelton applies this systematically to UAPs in The New Science of UFOs.
Exopsychology
Hazelton and Gilbert’s term for a systematic methodology of imagining non-human intelligence by taking every human characteristic and assuming the opposite: not animal-derived, not requiring food or water, possibly immortal, possibly non-social, possibly dark-matter-based. Deliberately defamiliarising.
Not Impossible
The operative epistemic standard throughout the episode. Distinguished from ‘likely’ or ‘probable’ — used specifically for hypotheses (time travel, ancient Earth civilisations, warp drives) that cannot be ruled out by current physics even if no evidence yet supports them. NASA’s Innovative Propulsion Program uses the same standard: they fund anything that isn’t impossible.
Sources and Methods
Intelligence community term for the classified collection systems and human assets used to gather intelligence. Protecting sources and methods from adversary knowledge is considered more important than almost any other consideration — including transparency about UAP sightings captured by those systems.
Microwave Hearing Effect (Frey Effect)
The documented ability to cause a person to “hear” sounds by beaming microwave radiation at them — the microwave causes thermoelastic expansion in the inner ear endolymph, producing vibrations experienced as sound. Real, laboratory-confirmed. Hazelton identifies this as one plausible Havana Syndrome mechanism.
Closed Timelike Curve
A path through spacetime that loops back to its own starting point in time — mathematically permissible in general relativity in the vicinity of a rapidly spinning (Kerr) black hole. Not physically achieved; not physically forbidden. Hazelton uses this as an existence proof that time travel isn’t ruled out by physics.
Alcubierre Drive
A theoretical faster-than-light propulsion method proposed by physicist Miguel Alcubierre in 1994: a bubble of spacetime contracts in front of the vessel and expands behind it, carrying the vessel at any velocity without the vessel itself exceeding c within its local frame. Requires negative energy, whose existence is unproven.
Diamagnetic Levitation
Levitation of non-magnetic objects using a sufficiently strong external magnetic field that repels the object’s diamagnetic response. Demonstrated with living frogs, birds, and insects. Relevant to UAP discussion as a potential propulsion mechanism requiring no onboard fuel.
Panspermia
The hypothesis that life or its chemical precursors (amino acids, nucleic acids) can travel between planets and star systems via comets, asteroids, or directed means. Supported by detection of nucleic acid bases on asteroid Bennu and amino acids on comets.
Von Neumann Probe
A theoretical self-replicating spacecraft that, upon reaching a target star system, uses local resources to build copies of itself and continue exploring. Proposed as the most efficient mechanism for exploring a galaxy. Hazelton argues any visiting craft is more likely an AI von Neumann probe than a crewed biological vessel.
