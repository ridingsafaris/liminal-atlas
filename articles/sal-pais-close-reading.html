Heaviside’s Reformulation of Maxwell

Maxwell’s original equations (1865) were written in quaternion mathematics — a four-dimensional number system — as 20 equations in 20 unknowns. They were physically complete but practically unusable for working engineers.

Heaviside’s reformulation (1880s) reduced these to four vector equations (∇·E, ∇×E, ∇·B, ∇×B) — what is taught today. This made the mathematics tractable but removed the scalar potential terms Pais and Dave Rossi argue are physically significant.

Harmonic oscillator: The simplest oscillating system (spring-mass, F = −kx), producing sinusoidal motion. Pais says coupling the Heaviside equations with the harmonic oscillator — the simplest possible physics — produces the relationships needed for the Pais Effect. This is what makes him confident the approach is correct: it emerges from elementary physics, not exotic new mathematics.

Technical Glossary

Pais Effect (P Effect)
Controlled motion of electrically charged objects — from solids to plasma — based on accelerated vibration and/or spin, producing extremely high electromagnetic energy densities. Named partly after Abraham Pais (Einstein’s biographer). The claimed mechanism behind all four Pais Navy patents. Not independently confirmed.
✗ No peer-review support
Appears in: Ch. 07, 08, 09, 11, 12, 33
Schwinger Limit
The critical electric field strength (~1.32 × 10¹⁸ V/m) at which the quantum vacuum becomes nonlinear — strong enough to spontaneously produce electron-positron pairs. A confirmed QED prediction (Schwinger, 1951), never achieved in a lab. Pais treats this as the threshold condition for the Pais Effect.
✓ Verified (QED prediction)
Appears in: Ch. 06, 09, 16
BCS Theory
Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer theory of conventional superconductivity (Nobel 1972). Below critical temperature, phonons mediate electron pairing (Cooper pairs) into a macroscopic quantum state with zero resistance and Meissner effect. Pais argues this passive, composition-dependent approach is superseded by his active superconductor concept.
✓ Verified mainstream theory
Appears in: Ch. 10, 11, 12
Bose-Einstein Plasma
A plasma (ionised gas) driven far from thermodynamic equilibrium that Pais claims can transition into a collective Bose-Einstein condensate state — exhibiting macroscopic quantum coherence and dramatically amplified EM effects. The target state for the Pais Effect device.
✗ No experimental confirmation
Appears in: Ch. 08, 09, 10
Super Force
The hypothetical unified fundamental force from which all four known forces emerged as the early universe cooled. At the Planck scale all forces theoretically unify. Pais claims UAP propulsion directly manipulates this pre-differentiated force — explaining why it bypasses both gravity and electromagnetism as normally described.
✗ Theoretical — no experimental access
Appears in: Ch. 03, 06, 09, 32
Lead Zirconate Titanate (PZT)
A piezoelectric ceramic (Pb[ZrᴵTi₁₋ᴵ]O₃) — generates voltage when mechanically stressed, deforms when voltage is applied. The most widely used piezoelectric industrial material. Pais chose it because lead, zirconium, and titanium are each superconducting at ultra-low temperatures, making PZT a candidate for his active superconductor approach via the bipolaron mechanism.
△ Material confirmed; active superconductor claim disputed
Appears in: Ch. 12, 13
Torsion / Einstein-Cartan Gravity
In Einstein-Cartan theory (a GR extension), the spin angular momentum of matter acts as a source of spacetime torsion — a twisting distinct from curvature. Jack argues that amplifying spin density via the Pais Effect could produce macroscopic torsional fields that couple to gravity, enabling lab-scale gravity manipulation.
△ Einstein-Cartan is real; macro-torsion from Pais Effect is speculative
