Technical Terms: Extended Electrodynamics

Extended electrodynamics / More Complete Electrodynamics (MCE): Theoretical frameworks extending the Heaviside-reformulated Maxwell equations by restoring scalar potential terms removed by the Lorenz gauge condition. U1 model / flat space-time: The U(1) gauge symmetry group underlying standard quantum electrodynamics assumes a flat Minkowski space-time metric. Rossi argues that engineering departures from this assumption — curving space-time locally via electromagnetic means — unlocks non-standard wave phenomena.

Physics Explainer: Transverse vs Longitudinal Waves & the Scalar Field

Transverse electromagnetic waves (light, radio, microwave): The electric field E and magnetic field B oscillate perpendicular (⊥) to each other and to the direction of propagation. This is the only free-space EM wave solution in standard Maxwell theory under the Lorenz gauge.

Longitudinal waves are real and well-understood in acoustics (sound), seismology (P-waves), and plasma physics (Langmuir waves). In longitudinal propagation, the disturbance moves parallel (∥) to the direction of travel — as in a compressed slinky. The controversy is whether this mode exists for electromagnetic waves in free space. In extended electrodynamics frameworks (Zohuri, Barrett, Bearden), removing the Lorenz gauge constraint produces longitudinal EM solutions. No peer-reviewed independently replicated experiment has confirmed free-space EM longitudinal propagation as of 2025.

The Aharonov-Bohm effect (1959, confirmed by Tonomura et al. 1986): An electron in a field-free region near a magnetic solenoid is phase-shifted by the magnetic vector potential A, even though the measurable fields B and E are zero in the electron’s path. This proves electromagnetic potentials are physically real, not merely mathematical conveniences. Rossi uses this as the empirical anchor for his claim that the scalar and vector potentials — not E and B fields — are the fundamental physical objects.

Physics Explainer: Cold Electricity, Negentropy and Cold Fusion

Cold electricity: Electrical effects that cool rather than heat conductors and surrounding material. Tesla claimed to produce cold electrical currents through high-frequency resonance experiments. In standard physics all current in resistive conductors produces Joule heat (P = I²R). Cold electrical effects, if real, require a different energy pathway — which Rossi attributes to the curved space-time framework: in locally curved space-time, the standard U(1) flat-space thermodynamic predictions change.

Negentropy: Standard thermodynamics requires entropy to increase in closed systems (second law). Rossi argues curved space-time creates a locally open system exchanging energy with the quantum vacuum, so the second law breaks down locally — allowing energy output to exceed input.

Cold fusion / LENR: Rossi connects curved space-time to cold-fusion-like phenomena by arguing that scalar potential manipulation could reduce the Coulomb barrier between nuclei — the electrostatic repulsion preventing nuclear fusion at low temperatures. If local space-time geometry can be modified electromagnetically, the effective potential well nuclei must overcome could theoretically be reduced. Pons and Fleischmann (1989) reported anomalous heat from palladium electrolytic cells — mainstream science attributed this to calorimetric error, but anomalous excess heat in similar systems has been replicated at SRI International, ENEA (Italy), and U.S. Navy SPAWAR and remains unexplained.

Caduceus geometry: The caduceus — two serpents wound around a staff — is Rossi’s geometric model for what curved space-time wave propagation looks like: two interlocking helical waves, analogous to the DNA double helix, propagating along a central axis. This is distinct from the medical caduceus (two snakes) vs the Rod of Asclepius (one snake) — Rossi uses the double-serpent image to represent the interleaving of electromagnetic and gravitational/scalar wave components.
