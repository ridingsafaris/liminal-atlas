Physics Explainer: Maxwell → Heaviside → Extended Electrodynamics

Maxwell (1865): 20 equations in 20 unknowns using quaternion mathematics. Scalar potential (φ) and vector potential (A) were first-class physical objects.

Heaviside & Gibbs (1880s): Reformulated into 4 modern vector equations, applying the Lorenz gauge condition (∂φ/∂t + ∇·A = 0). This eliminates longitudinal wave solutions from free space. Valid for all known laboratory phenomena.

Extended electrodynamics (MCE): Removes or relaxes the gauge constraint, generating additional “scalar longitudinal wave” (SLW) solutions. As of 2025, no peer-reviewed independently replicated experiment confirms these. QED — tested to 1 part in 10¹² — has no room for such effects.

Aharonov-Bohm effect (Aharonov & Bohm, 1959; confirmed Tonomura et al., 1986): An electron in a field-free region near a solenoid is phase-shifted by the magnetic vector potential A — even though B=0 there. Potentials have physical consequences. Rossi uses this as his foundation.

Physics Explainer: DC vs AC Gravitational Fields

DC gravity (static/constant): The familiar gravitational field — constant, unvarying, pulling objects toward a mass. Earth’s gravitational field is effectively DC at human scales. In gravitational wave physics, a static mass produces no radiation — you need changing mass distributions or accelerating masses to emit gravitational waves. General Relativity’s static field solutions (Schwarzschild, Kerr) describe DC-type fields.

AC gravity (alternating/pulsed): A time-varying gravitational field — analogous to alternating current in electrical systems. Gravitational waves, confirmed by LIGO in 2015, are ripples in space-time that propagate at the speed of light — these are AC gravitational phenomena. The distinction Rossi draws is between a superconductor operating in a continuous rotation mode (producing a roughly static gravitomagnetic field — “DC”) versus a pulsed, oscillating mode (producing high-frequency gravitational waves — “AC”).

Ning Li and Douglas Torr’s 1993 paper in Foundations of Physics Letters, titled “Gravito-Electric Coupling via Superconductivity”, proposed that time-varying applied magnetic fields could induce both gravitomagnetic (DC-type) and gravitoelectric (AC-type) fields in the superconducting lattice. The name AC Gravity LLC — Li’s post-university company, DoD-funded from 2001 — refers specifically to the alternating component of this proposed effect.

Tesla’s foundational insight was that alternating current — current that oscillates in direction at a set frequency — was far more useful than direct current for power transmission, because it could be stepped up and down by transformers and because its oscillating nature allowed for resonance effects impossible with DC. Tesla’s Wardenclyffe Tower project (1901–1917) was built on the principle that resonant Earth frequencies — longitudinal oscillations through the planet — could transmit energy globally. The Townsend Brown Connection: High Voltage and Gravitational Asymmetry

Thomas Townsend Brown’s discovery of the Biefeld-Brown effect (1920s) — apparent thrust from high-voltage asymmetric capacitors — is directly relevant to the AC/DC gravity framework. Brown observed that when a high-voltage DC field was applied to an asymmetric capacitor, the device moved toward the positive pole. When he switched to pulsed (AC) high voltage, the effects changed in character: the thrust became frequency-dependent.

Brown spent decades arguing that what he had discovered was not an electromagnetic artefact but a direct coupling between electrical potential and gravitational potential — that high electric field gradients could locally modify gravity. His 1952 paper “Electrogravitics Systems: A New Concept of Flight” and the U.S. Navy’s Project Winterhaven explicitly investigated whether pulsed high-voltage systems could produce AC-type gravitational effects for propulsion.

Rossi’s model extends this: where Brown used high-voltage DC and saw limited, disputed effects, Rossi argues that superconducting systems pulsed at the right frequency produce the AC gravitational field with far greater efficiency. The reason, in his framework, is that a superconductor’s quantum coherence allows the entire lattice — not just surface charge — to participate in the gravitomagnetic coupling. Brown was pointing at the same phenomenon with less efficient technology.

Ning Li & Douglas Torr — Superconductors and Gravity (1991–1993)
Li & Torr 1991 · Physical Review D

Li, N. and Torr, D.G. (1991). “Effects of a Gravitomagnetic Field on Pure Superconductors.” Physical Review D, 43(2), 457–459.

The foundational paper proposing that a rotating superconducting lattice could generate a detectable gravitomagnetic field — the theoretical basis for Rossi’s entire discussion of superconductor-based gravity manipulation.

↗ DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevD.43.457
Li & Torr 1992 · Physical Review B

Li, N. and Torr, D.G. (1992). “Gravitational Effects on the Magnetic Attenuation of Superconductors.” Physical Review B, 46(9), 5489–5495.

Extension of the 1991 paper, modelling how gravitational coupling affects the Meissner effect. Introduced the sigma-mu framework suggesting gravitational effects are non-negligible in superconductors.

↗ DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevB.46.5489
Torr & Li 1993 · Foundations of Physics Letters

Torr, D.G. and Li, N. (1993). “Gravitoelectric-Electric Coupling via Superconductivity.” Foundations of Physics Letters, 6(4), 371–383.

The paper that explicitly proposes both a DC-type gravitomagnetic field and an AC-type gravitoelectric field from time-varying applied magnetic vector potentials in superconductors — the direct source of Rossi’s AC/DC gravity framework. The name “AC Gravity LLC” derives from the alternating gravitoelectric component described here.

↗ DOI: 10.1007/BF00665654
Li et al. 1997 · Physica C

Li, N., Noever, D., Robertson, T., Koczor, R. and Brantley, W. (1997). “Static Test for a Gravitational Force Coupled to Type II YBCO Superconductors.” Physica C: Superconductivity, 281(2–3), 260–267.

NASA Marshall Space Flight Center experimental paper testing for gravitational effects in non-rotating superconductors. Found no effect with non-rotating disc, noting this was consistent with Podkletnov’s rotation-dependent results.

↗ DOI: 10.1016/S0921-4534(97)01462-7
Eugene Podkletnov — Gravitational Shielding (1992–2001)
Podkletnov & Nieminen 1992 · Physica C

Podkletnov, E. and Nieminen, R. (1992). “A Possibility of Gravitational Force Shielding by Bulk YBa₂Cu₃O₇‑x Superconductor.” Physica C: Superconductivity, 203(3–4), 441–444.

The original paper claiming 0.05–0.3% weight reduction in test masses above a rotating YBCO superconductor disc. Published, leaked to press, and withdrawn from peer review in 1996 — the paper that ignited the international scramble to replicate superconductor-gravity effects.

↗ DOI: 10.1016/0921-4534(92)90055-H
Podkletnov 1997 · arXiv preprint

Podkletnov, E.E. (1997). “Weak Gravitational Shielding Properties of Composite Bulk YBa₂Cu₃O₇‑x Superconductor below 70K under EM Field.” arXiv preprint cond-mat/9701074.

Updated experimental data posted to the arXiv preprint server after the 1992 paper was withdrawn from peer review. Reports weight reductions of up to 2.1%.

↗ arXiv: cond-mat/9701074
Podkletnov & Modanese 2001 · arXiv preprint

Podkletnov, E. and Modanese, G. (2001). “Impulse Gravity Generator Based on Charged YBa₂Cu₃O₇‑y Superconductor with Composite Crystal Structure.” arXiv preprint physics/0108005.

Description of the proposed “impulse gravity generator” which reportedly crushed brick and warped steel. Not peer-reviewed. Referenced by Rossi in relation to pulsed (AC-type) gravitational wave generation from superconductors.

↗ arXiv: physics/0108005
Salvatore Cezar Pais — U.S. Navy Patents & Publications (2016–2020)
Pais 2018 · U.S. Patent US10144532B2

Pais, S.C. (filed 28 April 2016; granted 4 December 2018). “Craft Using an Inertial Mass Reduction Device.” U.S. Patent No. US10144532B2. Assigned to the Department of the Navy.

Describes a hybrid aerospace-undersea craft enclosed in a polarised vacuum plasma sheath generated by high-frequency electromagnetic fields, theoretically reducing the craft’s inertial and gravitational mass coupling. Expired 9 January 2023.

↗ Google Patents: US10144532B2
Pais 2019 · U.S. Patent US10322827B2

Pais, S.C. (filed 14 February 2017; granted 18 June 2019). “High Frequency Gravitational Wave Generator.” U.S. Patent No. US10322827B2. Assigned to the Department of the Navy.

Describes a device using counter-spinning gas-filled cavities and microwave emitters to generate propagating gravitational field fluctuations — the AC gravity device in Rossi’s framework. Subsequently expired due to non-payment of maintenance fees.

↗ Google Patents: US10322827B2
Pais 2019 · IEEE Transactions on Plasma Science

Pais, S.C. (2019). “The Plasma Compression Fusion Device — Enabling Nuclear Fusion Ignition.” IEEE Transactions on Plasma Science, 47(11), 5119–5124.

The only peer-reviewed journal publication by Pais. Describes a compact fusion reactor concept. Referenced by Rossi as evidence that the underlying EM framework of the Pais Effect has received mainstream academic acceptance.

↗ DOI: 10.1109/TPS.2019.2942997
Pais 2017 · SAE Technical Papers

Pais, S.C. (2017). “High Frequency Gravitational Waves – Induced Propulsion.” SAE Technical Paper Series, 2017-01-2040.

Conference paper outlining the theoretical basis for using high-frequency gravitational waves as a propulsion mechanism. Presented at the SAE 2017 AeroTech Congress.

↗ DOI: 10.4271/2017-01-2040
Luc Montagnier — DNA Electromagnetic Signals (2009–2015)
Montagnier et al. 2009 · Interdisciplinary Sciences

Montagnier, L., Aïssa, J., Ferris, S., Montagnier, J-L. and Lavallée, C. (2009). “Electromagnetic Signals Are Produced by Aqueous Nanostructures Derived from Bacterial DNA Sequences.” Interdisciplinary Sciences: Computational Life Sciences, 1(2), 81–90.

The original paper claiming that bacterial DNA sequences emit detectable low-frequency EM signals when highly diluted in water. Published in a journal of which Montagnier was editor-in-chief. No independent replication has succeeded.

↗ DOI: 10.1007/s12539-009-0036-7 ↗ PubMed: 20640822
Montagnier et al. 2015 · Electromagnetic Biology and Medicine

Montagnier, L., Del Giudice, E., Aïssa, J., Lavallee, C., Motschwiller, S., Capolupo, A., Polcari, A., Romano, P., Tedeschi, A. and Vitiello, G. (2015). “Transduction of DNA Information through Water and Electromagnetic Waves.” Electromagnetic Biology and Medicine, 34(2), 106–112.

The 2015 follow-up paper extending the 2009 claims, claiming EM transduction has also been observed in living human cells exposed to EMS irradiation. Available as arXiv preprint 1501.01620.

↗ DOI: 10.3109/15368378.2015.1036072 ↗ arXiv: 1501.01620
Puthoff, Haisch & Rueda — Zero-Point Energy & Inertia (1994)
Haisch, Rueda & Puthoff 1994 · Physical Review A

Haisch, B., Rueda, A. and Puthoff, H.E. (1994). “Inertia as a Zero-Point-Field Lorentz Force.” Physical Review A, 49(2), 678–694.

Proposes that inertia — matter’s resistance to acceleration — is not an intrinsic property but an electromagnetic drag force arising from interaction with the quantum vacuum zero-point field (ZPF). The theoretical framework underlying Rossi’s claim that inertial mass can be reduced by manipulating local vacuum conditions. Subsequent analyses by Levin (2009) disputed the calculation.

↗ DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevA.49.678 ↗ PubMed: 9910287

Extended Electrodynamics & Scalar Wave Theory
Maxwell 1865 · Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society

Maxwell, J.C. (1865). “A Dynamical Theory of the Electromagnetic Field.” Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society of London, 155, 459–512.

The original 1865 paper presenting Maxwell’s full unified electromagnetic theory in 20 equations — the starting point for all extended electrodynamics arguments about what was lost in the Heaviside reformulation.

↗ DOI: 10.1098/rstl.1865.0008 ↗ Royal Society Publishing
Aharonov & Bohm 1959 · Physical Review

Aharonov, Y. and Bohm, D. (1959). “Significance of Electromagnetic Potentials in the Quantum Theory.” Physical Review, 115(3), 485–491.

The theoretical prediction that electromagnetic potentials (A and φ) have physically measurable effects even in regions where E and B fields are zero — confirmed experimentally by Tonomura et al. (1986). Rossi cites this as proof that scalar potentials are physically real, not mathematical conveniences.

↗ DOI: 10.1103/PhysRev.115.485
Tonomura et al. 1986 · Physical Review Letters

Tonomura, A., Osakabe, N., Matsuda, T., Kawasaki, T., Endo, J., Yano, S. and Yamada, H. (1986). “Evidence for Aharonov-Bohm Effect with Magnetic Field Completely Shielded from Electron Wave.” Physical Review Letters, 56(8), 792–795.

The definitive experimental confirmation of the Aharonov-Bohm effect using electron holography with a superconducting-shielded toroid. Established conclusively that electromagnetic potentials are physically real.

↗ DOI: 10.1103/PhysRevLett.56.792
