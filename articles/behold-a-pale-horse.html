William Cooper
Behold a Pale Horse

Author: Milton William “Bill” Cooper Published: 1991 · Light Technology Publishing 500 pages · 17 chapters · 7 appendices 9 chapters · 27 subsequent-view annotations

Behold a Pale Horse is the most widely circulated underground text of the late 20th century — one of the most shoplifted books in American publishing history, the unofficial manifesto of the militia movement, and foundational reading in hip-hop culture. Written by Navy veteran Bill Cooper, it weaves together documented institutional facts (the CFR, FEMA emergency powers, REX 84, Oliver North's Iran-Contra admissions) with unverifiable first-hand claims (the Eisenhower alien treaty, MJ-12) and demonstrable fabrications (the Protocols of the Elders of Zion, the AIDS bioweapon mechanism). Cooper himself admitted mixing true and false information and acknowledged he could not always tell them apart. This close reading covers each major claim section by section, with green / amber / red annotations tracking what has since been confirmed, refuted, or contextualised. Cooper is referenced — directly or through the ideas he popularised — across multiple Liminal Atlas close readings. This article is the reference page for those connections.

⚠ Note on Chapter 15

This book contains The Protocols of the Elders of Zion in full — a proven antisemitic forgery. Cooper reframed it as an Illuminati document. Later editions removed the chapter. We cover it here because understanding how it re-entered circulation through this book is historically important. The Protocols are a fabrication; that finding is not contested.

CH 01

Foreword — Cooper's Background & Stated Method

📋
Chapter Summary
✦ Claude AI Derived Summaries

Cooper establishes his credentials: he claims to have served on the Commander in Chief of the Pacific Fleet (CINCPACFLT) Intelligence Briefing Team, where he read Top Secret documents about the alien presence, MJ-12, and a secret government. ✗ Unverifiable His documented Navy rank was E-5 (petty officer second class) — a level incompatible with the security clearances he describes. ✓ Verified

Critically, Cooper admits in the same foreword that he deliberately fabricated information — mixing it with true information to feed to researchers he believed were government disinformation agents (Bill Moore, Stanton Friedman). He writes: "None of that information was intended to go to the public." Spieser, who was supposed to pass the material only to Moore, posted it publicly. ✓ Verified This is the most important epistemological fact about the book: Cooper himself confirms that corrupted information is present throughout, and acknowledges he cannot always distinguish it from the real material.

▼ Contradicts — Mark Jacobson, Pale Horse Rider (2018)
Jacobson's biography is the only serious attempt to verify Cooper's background. He found Cooper's Navy service confirmed but at E-5 rank — a low-level clerical position. No evidence of the security clearances needed to access the materials Cooper described. Jacobson's conclusion: Cooper was a genuine believer in what he said, not a conscious fraudster — but his 'documents' evolved over years to fit whatever he was currently arguing.

▼ Contradicts — Cooper's own 1989 public statement
Cooper publicly stated in 1989 that Stanton Friedman and Bill Moore were government agents and that the Eisenhower MJ-12 briefing document was a fraud. He later incorporated MJ-12 into his narrative anyway. This reversal is his own, not a critic's imposition.

CH 02

Chapter 1 — Silent Weapons for Quiet Wars

📋
Chapter Summary
✦ Claude AI Derived Summaries

Cooper reproduces a document he says was found in an IBM photocopier purchased at a military surplus sale. Titled Silent Weapons for Quiet Wars: An Introductory Programming Manual, it claims to be a 1954 Bilderberg Group policy document outlining economic and social control of populations through manipulation of money supply, media, and education — what it calls "silent weapons." Cooper frames it as the Illuminati's declaration of war on the American people. ✗ Unverifiable

The document contains sophisticated economic circuit diagrams that Cooper's version omits entirely — which is one reason researchers concluded he received it from elsewhere rather than authored it. The economic modelling it contains exceeds Cooper's stated expertise. △ Disputed

▼ Contradicts — Lyle Hartford Van Dyke Jr. (1990s)
Van Dyke, who was incarcerated, emerged claiming authorship — calling it 'a collage of ideas I compiled, not an original work.' This is the most specific attribution available but remains unverifiable. Hartford Van Dyke was released from federal prison in 2011.

■ Contextualises — The Skeptic (UK), 2023
Analysis confirmed that large parts of the book — including this chapter — were not written by Cooper. 'Chapters 1, 3, 4, 10, 14, 15, and 17, plus all of the appendices, were written by other people.' Cooper is the compiler and framer of Silent Weapons for Quiet Wars, not its author.

■ Contextualises — Paranoia Magazine analysis (2013)
Comparison of Cooper's stripped version with the full original pamphlet (which includes diagrams Cooper omitted) concluded he received the document from a third party. The presence of sophisticated economic mathematics beyond Cooper's expertise is cited as evidence he did not originate it. Whether the document is itself a hoax — and by whom — remains unresolved.

CH 03

Chapter 2 — Secret Societies and the New World Order

📋
Chapter Summary
✦ Claude AI Derived Summaries

Cooper maps a network connecting: Illuminati → Skull & Bones / Scroll & Key → Council on Foreign Relations (CFR) → Trilateral Commission → JASON Society → MJ-12. He claims every CIA director has been a CFR member, and that this network is pursuing a one-world government. △ Disputed

Documented facts embedded throughout: The CFR is real and influential. ✓ Verified The JASON Group is real — a classified scientific advisory body administered by the Mitre Corporation, with protocol rank of rear admiral when visiting military bases. ✓ Verified Its membership has been partially confirmed through Pentagon Papers and other sources; Cooper's claim that it is the highest degree of an occult order called the JASON Society is unverifiable. ✗ Unverifiable He names Dr. Edward Teller as a member of MJ-12 — Teller's proximity to classified government science is independently documented. △ Disputed

Cooper also claims Pope John Paul II was formerly a Nazi cyanide gas salesman involved in creating AIDS. This is historically false. ⚠ Known fabrication

▲ Supports — CFR membership — mainstream scholarship
Laurence Shoup's Wall Street's Think Tank (2015) documents the CFR's role in shaping US foreign policy through rigorous academic analysis — reaching similar conclusions about elite coordination to Cooper's, without the occult superstructure. The CFR's influence is not a fringe claim.

■ Contextualises — Michael Barkun, A Culture of Conspiracy (2003)
Political scientist Barkun characterises Behold a Pale Horse as 'among the most complex superconspiracy theories' and among the most influential. His analysis: Cooper fuses verifiable institutional facts (CFR, Trilateral Commission) with unverifiable occult superstructure (JASON Society, Illuminati) in a way that makes the whole package feel confirmed by the verified parts.

CH 04

Chapter 12 — The Secret Government (MJ-12, Alien Treaty, EBE)

📋
Chapter Summary
✦ Claude AI Derived Summaries

This is the book's most consequential chapter — the one that most directly shaped subsequent UFO disclosure discourse. Cooper's claims:

The Eisenhower Alien Treaty (1954): President Eisenhower met with an alien nation at Edwards Air Force Base on February 20, 1954. A formal treaty was signed: the aliens could abduct humans for medical examination and provide technology; in exchange the US would keep their presence secret. ✗ Unverifiable

MJ-12 (Majesty Twelve): A secret 19-member committee established by Eisenhower. Members included Nelson Rockefeller, Allen Dulles, John Foster Dulles, J. Edgar Hoover, and Dr. Edward Teller. The committee operated outside congressional oversight as the core of what Cooper calls the Secret Government. ✗ Unverifiable

EBE (Extraterrestrial Biological Entity): A live alien survivor of the 1949 Roswell crash, held in captivity, dying of illness in 1952. The film E.T. is described as a thinly disguised account. ✗ Unverifiable

The Alien Deception: By 1955, the aliens had broken the treaty — mutilating humans, not providing complete abductee lists, and manipulating humanity through secret societies, religion, and the occult. ✗ Unverifiable

Underground Bases: Jointly occupied alien-human bases beneath the Four Corners area and at Area 51 (Dreamland), housing at least 600 alien beings full time alongside CIA personnel. ✗ Unverifiable

James Forrestal murdered: The first Secretary of Defence was strangled with a bedsheet and thrown from a window at Bethesda Naval Hospital by CIA agents because he wanted to inform the public about the aliens. ✗ Unverifiable

Cooper himself writes: "Whether this is true or disinformation depends solely upon the existence of aliens." He cannot verify it independently.

▼ Contradicts — The MJ-12 documents — FBI investigation
The FBI investigated the MJ-12 documents and concluded they were fabrications. The Air Force Office of Special Investigations reached the same conclusion. The key anachronism: the typewriter font used in the Eisenhower Briefing Document did not exist in 1952, providing a hard physical date-of-fabrication tell. Academic consensus is firmly against the documents' authenticity.

▼ Contradicts — Bill Moore's 1989 MUFON confession
Bill Moore — one of the MJ-12 documents' main distributors and a figure Cooper called a 'government agent' — confessed at the 1989 MUFON symposium that he had knowingly spread disinformation as part of a 'work relationship' with Air Force intelligence. He also confirmed the Paul Benowitz operation — deliberately feeding false UFO information to a researcher until he suffered a breakdown. Documented in Mark Pilkington's Mirage Men.

■ Contextualises — David Grusch testimony (2023)
Grusch's Congressional testimony confirmed the existence of legacy UAP crash retrieval programmes but did not name MJ-12. His confirmed existence of reverse-engineering programmes with unaccountable funding structures rhymes with Cooper's general architecture, approached through a completely different evidential path. Grusch was a USAF intelligence officer with documented clearances — the access Cooper only claimed.

■ Contextualises — James Forrestal — historical record
Forrestal died at Bethesda Naval Hospital on May 22, 1949 — officially ruled a suicide. Cooper's CIA murder claim is unverifiable, but the case has genuine anomalies noted by historians. His brother had visited the day before and found him in good spirits. Jorjani also flags Forrestal's death as suspicious, connecting it to his knowledge of Operation High Jump. The questions are not frivolous, but Cooper's specific CIA-bedsheet-window mechanism is unsupported.

CH 05

Chapter 15 — The Protocols of the Elders of Zion

📋
Chapter Summary
✦ Claude AI Derived Summaries

Cooper reproduces the entirety of The Protocols of the Elders of Zion — an early 20th century antisemitic forgery describing a supposed Jewish conspiracy to dominate the world. Cooper instructs readers to replace "Zion" with "Sion", "Jews" with "Illuminati", and "goyim" with "cattle." He claims the Protocols were written by the Prieuré de Sion to describe Illuminati plans, and that Jews were falsely inserted as the villains. ⚠ Known fabrication

⚠ Editorial Note — Liminal Atlas

The Protocols of the Elders of Zion is one of the most thoroughly debunked documents in modern history — proven to be a plagiarised fabrication by the Okhrana (Russian Tsarist secret police) in 1903, drawn from an 1864 French political satire by Maurice Joly. Its forgery was demonstrated conclusively in August 1921 by Philip Graves in The Times of London. Cooper's 'substitution' instruction does not change the text — it reframes a known antisemitic fabrication as an Illuminati blueprint, and in doing so gave the document renewed circulation in communities that would not have otherwise encountered it. Later printings of Behold a Pale Horse removed this chapter. We cover it here because its inclusion explains the document's re-emergence in conspiracy circuits in the 1990s and beyond.

▼ Contradicts — Graves exposé, The Times of London (1921)
Philip Graves proved in August 1921 that the Protocols were plagiarised word-for-word in multiple passages from Maurice Joly's 1864 satirical novel Dialogue in Hell Between Machiavelli and Montesquieu. This original debunking has never been credibly contested.

▼ Contradicts — Norman Cohn, Warrant for Genocide (1967)
Cohn's definitive historical study traces the Protocols from Okhrana fabrication through their use in Nazi propaganda. The scholarly consensus on their fraudulent origin is unanimous across all serious historians.

▼ Contradicts — Real-world consequences
In 2000, South Africa's Minister of Health Manto Tshabalala-Msimang distributed Cooper's Protocols chapter to senior government officials. Researchers including Nicoli Nattrass documented how this contributed to AIDS denialism policies that cost lives — a direct downstream consequence of Cooper's repackaging of the forgery.

CH 06

Appendix D — AIDS as Bioweapon

📋
Chapter Summary
✦ Claude AI Derived Summaries

Cooper argues AIDS was created as a population-reduction tool targeting Blacks, Hispanics, and gay men. His chain of claims: (1) In 1969 the US Department of Defense requested $10 million to create a synthetic biological agent that would destroy the immune system. △ Disputed (2) A hepatitis B vaccine trial in New York (1978–81) was the delivery mechanism. △ Disputed (3) The mastermind was Dr. Wolf Szmuness, who Cooper claims was the ex-roommate of Pope John Paul II. ⚠ Known fabrication

The 1969 Congressional Record statement is real — a Congressman did oppose a biological warfare programme. ✓ Verified The hepatitis B trials in gay men (1978–81) are historically documented. ✓ Verified Cooper's interpretation — that these funded and delivered HIV — is not supported by molecular evidence. △ Disputed

▼ Contradicts — Scientific consensus on HIV origin
HIV-1 is thoroughly documented as having crossed from chimpanzees to humans in approximately 1908 in Cameroon (Worobey et al., 2008, Nature). Phylogenetic analysis of preserved samples confirms natural zoonotic origin predating any laboratory programme. No credible molecular evidence of engineering has been found.

▼ Contradicts — The 1969 Congressional Record
The statement Cooper cites is real but does not say what he claims. The Congressman was opposing biological warfare programmes generally — not funding the creation of AIDS specifically. Cooper extrapolated the AIDS connection without documentary evidence.

■ Contextualises — The Tuskegee context
The conspiracy theory's persistence reflects real historical injustices — the Tuskegee syphilis study (1932–1972), in which the US government deliberately withheld treatment from Black men — that give it plausibility in communities with genuine reason to distrust official medicine. This context does not validate Cooper's specific claims but explains their reception.

CH 07

Chapters 6–8 — FEMA & Emergency Powers

📋
Chapter Summary
✦ Claude AI Derived Summaries

Cooper documents the executive order infrastructure giving FEMA extraordinary emergency powers: EO 10995 (communications), EO 10997 (energy and minerals), EO 11000 (civilian labour brigades), EO 11490 (Nixon consolidation), Carter's EO 12148. He argues these constitute a hidden infrastructure for suspending the Constitution under any declared national emergency. △ Disputed

He also covers REX 84 — a 1984 readiness exercise testing government response to civil unrest — and H.R. 4079, a proposed drug emergency declaration. The executive orders are real and publicly documented. ✓ Verified Oliver North confirmed in the Iran-Contra hearings that plans existed to suspend the Constitution. ✓ Verified

▲ Supports — Oliver North confirmation (1987)
North confirmed under oath during the Iran-Contra hearings that he had been involved in developing continuity-of-government plans that included suspending the Constitution. Congressman Jack Brooks (D-TX) was cut off by committee chairman Daniel Inouye when he tried to pursue this line of questioning. This is one of Cooper's most solidly documented claims.

▲ Supports — REX 84 — confirmed
REX 84 (Readiness Exercise 1984) was confirmed as real by documents obtained by the Miami Herald and discussed in the Iran-Contra hearings. It involved planning for the detention of large numbers of undocumented immigrants and American citizens in the event of a national emergency.

■ Contextualises — Post-9/11 architecture
The USA PATRIOT Act (2001), the creation of the Department of Homeland Security, and subsequent executive expansions of surveillance power closely parallel the emergency architecture Cooper described. This doesn't confirm his specific conspiracy claims but validates the general concern that emergency frameworks were being built that could bypass constitutional constraints.

CH 08

Chapter 2 (partial) — JFK Assassination

📋
Chapter Summary
✦ Claude AI Derived Summaries

Cooper claims Kennedy was assassinated because he was about to reveal the alien presence. The mechanism: Secret Service agent William Greer (the driver) fired the fatal shot using a "gas pressure device developed by aliens from the Trilateral Commission." Cooper circulated a video purporting to prove this. ⚠ Known fabrication

▼ Contradicts — Video analysis — multiple stations
Multiple television stations that examined Cooper's video concluded it was fabricated from existing Zapruder footage. The frames purportedly showing Greer firing were created by manipulation of frames in which Greer simply turned to look at Kennedy after the first shot.

▼ Contradicts — House Select Committee on Assassinations (1979)
While finding a probable conspiracy based on acoustic evidence (suggesting a second shooter on the grassy knoll), the HSCA did not identify the driver as a shooter. No serious JFK researcher across the political spectrum has credited the Greer-alien-weapon theory.

CH 09

Legacy — The 9/11 Prediction & Cultural Impact

📋
Chapter Summary
✦ Claude AI Derived Summaries

On June 28, 2001, Cooper broadcast: "Whatever they're going to blame on Usama bin Laden — don't you believe it… They will soon do something outlandish to gain the support of the sheeple." He referenced a CNN report from June 24 in which bin Laden had vowed to attack US interests. Three months later, 9/11 occurred. ✓ Verified

Whether this constitutes prophecy or extrapolation from public reporting is genuinely contested. Bin Laden had been a named terrorist since the late 1990s, with his own CIA unit since 1996, and had carried out the USS Cole bombing ten months earlier. The "prediction" contained no date, method, or specific target. △ Disputed

Cooper was killed on November 5, 2001 — two months after 9/11 — in a shootout with Apache County sheriff's deputies who had come to arrest him on outstanding aggravated assault charges. He had publicly vowed he would not be taken alive. One deputy was shot in the head (survived); Cooper was killed. ✓ Verified

Cultural reach: Behold a Pale Horse is one of the most shoplifted books in American publishing history. ✓ Verified It circulated heavily in US prisons throughout the 1990s, particularly in Black communities. It is cited by Wu-Tang Clan affiliates (Killarmy named their 1997 debut album Silent Weapons for Quiet Wars), Public Enemy, and Ice Cube. Timothy McVeigh ordered Cooper materials before the Oklahoma City bombing. Alex Jones has cited Cooper extensively, though Cooper publicly despised Jones. ✓ Verified

■ Contextualises — Mark Jacobson, Pale Horse Rider (2018)
The definitive biography. Jacobson traces the book's spread through prisons, barbershops, militia compounds, and hip-hop circles. He argues Cooper was 'the man who saw the deep state before it had a name' — a figure whose genuine disillusionment with government legitimised a framework that mixed true institutional critique with irresponsible fabrication.

■ Contextualises — The Skeptic (UK), 2023
'Why inaccuracies in Bill Cooper's influential conspiracy theory book serve as its strength': the mixture of documented facts with false claims is not a weakness but the mechanism of the book's persuasion — each true fact borrows credibility to the false claims around it, and every debunking of a false claim can be reframed as confirmation that the cover-up is working.

■ Contextualises — QAnon lineage
Media scholars including Adrienne LaFrance (The Atlantic, 2020) traced QAnon's mythology directly to Cooper's framework: the secret government, satanic child abuse in elite networks, sealed indictments, the deep state. These are Cooper's architecture, updated for the social media age. Cooper himself would likely have disavowed QAnon, as he publicly despised imitators.
