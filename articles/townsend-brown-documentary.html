BIEFELD-BROWN EFFECT ▶ 0:08:49 The Biefeld-Brown Effect — Anomalous Physics at the Heart of the Story
The core experimental principle. Place a neutral insulator (dielectric) between two metal plates — one positive, one negative, with the positive plate smaller than the negative. This asymmetric capacitor, when charged with megavolt-range direct current, causes the negative plate to “chase” the positive plate — producing thrust even when the positive plate is oriented skyward, appearing to beat gravity. △ contested The controversy: critics attribute the thrust to ion wind — ambient air molecules ionised by the electric field creating aerodynamic pressure. Brown said this was wrong: the effect persisted in a vacuum. The consistent theme across Brown’s entire career: extremely high voltage, extremely low current. The Air Force’s 1990 vacuum test used only 19kV — laughably below the megavoltage Brown required. △ contested The naming: Brown credits Paul Alfred Biefeld, an Ohio-based physicist who had studied alongside Einstein, with the capacitor idea. Detractors long claimed Biefeld and Brown never met — until an affidavit emerged signed by Paul Alfred Biefeld confirming the meeting and the anomalous effect. ✓ documented The 1956 Mont Gouffier vacuum tests: French Air Force officer Jacques Cornon (also a technical representative for aircraft manufacturer Sud-Ouest) facilitated Brown’s Paris experiments at the Mont Gouffier facility. Cornon died in 2008 aged 98, and Paul Shatkin interviewed him just before his death — still fully lucid. He confirmed the reports: the effect persisted in vacuum of at least 10⁻⁶ torr, with residual thrust “several orders of magnitude larger than the remaining ambient ionisation can account for.” ✓ documented Each successive demonstration at Mont Gouffier was attended by a higher-level French official — from Ministry of Science to generals to the French Atomic Energy Commission — indicating a genuine escalating reaction to what they were witnessing.
“In short, it appears there is strong evidence that the Biefeld-Brown effect does exist in the negative-to-positive direction in a vacuum of at least 10⁻⁶ torr. The residual thrust is several orders of magnitude larger than the remaining ambient ionisation can account for.”

1953 · Proposal · “Project Winterhaven”

The blueprint for a flying saucer programme using electrogravitics. Proposes a Mach 3 interceptor disc using the Biefeld-Brown effect. Jesse calls this the document that “almost exactly resembles how all insiders describe America’s secret UFO programme.” The 1956 Aviation Studies reports on electrogravitics explicitly reference Winterhaven as the origin point of the military industrial interest in electrogravitics.

Declassified Government Documents

1956 · Declassified · Aviation Studies (International) Ltd.

Aviation Studies (International) Ltd., Gravity Research Group, Special Weapons Study Unit (1956). (1) Electrogravitics Systems: A New Propulsion Methodology. (2) The Gravitics Situation. Both prepared February 1956. Classified by the US Air Force; later declassified.

The two most significant declassified documents on electrogravitics. Electrogravitics Systems defines electrogravitics as “a synthesis of electrostatic energy use for propulsion” and lists major US corporations collaborating on the research (Douglas, Glenn Martin, General Electric, Bell, Convair, Lear, Sperry-Rand). The Gravitics Situation shows the continuity from Project Winterhaven and proposes a Mach 3 saucer-shaped interceptor requiring a dielectric constant of 30,000. Both documents explicitly reference Brown’s work.
